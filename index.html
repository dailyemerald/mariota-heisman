MARIOTA FOR HEISMAN

Ever since Oregon quarterback Marcus Mariota announced that he would come back to school for his senior year at the University of Oregon, the 6-foot-4, 219-pound dual-threat athlete has been embedded in the Heisman Trophy conversation.

BY JUSTIN WISE

Preseason predictions immediately began to forecast Oregon as a national title contender and him as a sure bet to be the first pick in the 2015 Draft. As September approached, the media coverage on the reserved Hawaiian native was at a maximum. Seemingly each day something new about an individual who prefers his life to stay private was nationally publicized and just a few weeks into the regular season, his face was already gracing the cover of Sports Illustrated. It wasn’t as if that was a symbol of his march to fame though – considering the fact that it was the third time he appeared on one.

Heading into the 2014 season, Mariota’s resume was already fully decorated and his career stat line was expected to set a large variety of school and conference records. Then by the second week of the season, his preseason Heisman stock was already being validated. Playing in what had been dubbed as the best nonconference matchup of the season, Mariota passed for 318 yards and three touchdowns – adding 42 yards on the ground – while guiding Oregon to a comeback win over Michigan State, 46-27.
